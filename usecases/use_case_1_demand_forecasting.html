Are volatile demand cycles disrupting your production planning?

Consumer goods manufacturers face constant uncertainty from seasonality, promotional velocity, retailer behavior, macroeconomic shifts, and rapidly changing social trends. Traditional forecast models are slow, backward-looking, and frequently misaligned with actual consumption patterns, creating a cascade of costly consequences.

  • Excess Inventory & Write-Downs: Overproduction leads to bloated warehouses, product spoilage, high carrying costs, and significant end-of-lifecycle markdowns that erode margins.
  • Stockouts & Lost Revenue: Underproduction results in out-of-stock situations, retailer penalties, loss of valuable shelf space, and permanent market share erosion to competitors.
  • Reactive Planning Chaos: Sales, supply chain, and plant scheduling teams spend excessive time firefighting and reconciling conflicting Excel spreadsheets instead of strategic planning.
  • Poor Capital Allocation: Inaccurate forecasts force manufacturers to hold excess working capital in safety stock while simultaneously missing opportunities to capture demand spikes.
  • Disconnected Data Signals: Critical external signals like point-of-sale data, weather patterns, social sentiment, and promotional calendars remain siloed and underutilized in planning decisions.
  • Slow Response Times: By the time traditional models identify trends and teams manually adjust production schedules, market conditions have already shifted again.
