Is scheduling complexity limiting throughput?

Planners spend hours relying on tribal knowledge and spreadsheets to produce "good enough" schedules. This results in constant firefighting, lower throughput, and higher costs.

  • Complex Constraints: Changeover costs, allergens, batch sequences, and sanitation create thousands of non-linear combinations.
  • Inefficient Schedules: Manual planning often misses optimization opportunities, leading to lower line throughput.
  • High Setup Costs: Poor sequencing increases machine setup times and friction.
  • Cognitive Overload: Planners face constant stress trying to balance conflicting variables manually.
