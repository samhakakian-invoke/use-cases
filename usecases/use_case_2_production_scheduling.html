Is manual scheduling limiting your production potential?

Consumer goods manufacturing scheduling is extraordinarily complex with changeover costs, machine setup times, batch sequences, allergen controls, packaging line availability, and sanitation requirements creating thousands of interdependent constraints. Traditional methods leave significant value on the table.

  • Tribal Knowledge Dependency: Scheduling expertise resides in a few key individuals, creating vulnerability and making it nearly impossible to onboard new planners effectively.
  • Suboptimal Throughput: Manual scheduling produces "good enough" results rather than optimal solutions, leaving 5-15% of potential capacity unutilized due to inefficient sequencing.
  • Excessive Changeover Costs: Poor batch sequencing leads to unnecessary equipment changeovers, sanitation cycles, and setup time that could be minimized through intelligent optimization.
  • Constant Firefighting: Planners spend hours reacting to disruptions and manually adjusting schedules instead of focusing on strategic improvements and capacity planning.
  • Schedule Volatility: Frequent last-minute changes cascade through the plant, disrupting labor allocation, maintenance windows, and creating inefficiencies across all support functions.
  • Constraint Blind Spots: Spreadsheet-based planning cannot simultaneously optimize across all constraints like allergen controls, packaging compatibility, and capacity restrictions, leading to suboptimal decisions.
