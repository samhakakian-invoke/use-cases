Struggling with volatile demand cycles?

Traditional forecast models are slow and backward-looking, frequently misaligned with real consumption signals. This creates cascading effects on cost, capacity allocation, and working capital.

  • Volatile Demand: Seasonality, promotion velocity, and social trends create unpredictable swings in consumption.
  • Costly Overproduction: Leads to excess inventory, high warehousing costs, spoilage, and end-of-lifecycle write-downs.
  • Risky Underproduction: Results in out-of-stock situations, retailer penalties, lost shelf space, and lost market share.
  • Manual Friction: Planners are stuck doing manual Excel consolidation instead of strategic decision-making.
